About hopper.pw

What's this?

hopper.pw is a free dynamic DNS service, as well as an open source software project.

What the heck is (dynamic) DNS!?

DNS stands for Domain Name System. This is the "addressbook" of the internet: Every device in the internet has an IP address - but because people are bad at remembering numbers DNS translates easy to remember names to IP addresses.
Your router at home also is a device in the internet, so it has an IP address - but oh... it does NOT automatically have a DNS entry. Also your IP address changes regularly. To update a DNS entry most routers have the option to report their IP address to a dynamic DNS service (like hopper.pw) if it changed. So you can reach your home router from the internet at anytime (e.g. for remote administration or switching off the lights)

Where do I get a domain (DNS entry) from?

As part of the free service hopper.pw offers a subdomain (<something>.hopper.pw) for you. If you are more advanced, have your own domain and a nsupdate-capable DNS server you can add your own domain.

What does it cost?

Nothing! The service is completely free of charge. No hidden costs!

How is hopper.pw funded?

hopper.pw is completely funded from private pocket. If you want to support hopper.pw, keep the service running and help me pay for the server please consider donating or pressing the flattr button. If you don't want to support with money just spread the word or make your own contributions to the project.

Why another dyndns service?

Yes - there are already various other dyndns services. Buuuuut... We were annoyed by always having to click on a link in an email every couple of days to prevent the deletion of the domain on other widely spread services and also to always try finding the well hidden button for the free accounts. That's why we thought "let's host our own service - can't be to difficult!". So during djangodash 2013 nsupdate.info was born. After the dash the project split into two forks: nsupdate.info and hopper.pw.

Who is maintaining hopper.pw?

Hopper.pw is maintained and funded by Arne Schauf. The first version was developed on djangodash 2013.

Is it reliable?

I try to do my best to keep the service up and running.
But: This is a free service, there are no warranties. If it breaks, it breaks.
